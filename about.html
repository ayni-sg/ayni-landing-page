The Ayni Thesis

"Ayni" represents reciprocity—the concept of mutual aid, collective sharing, and thriving together locally.

In a world dominated by attention-extractive feeds and fragmented community forums, finding trustworthy interest groups, reliable peer-educators, or supporting a home bakery down your block has become increasingly transactional.

Ayni Social is engineered to restore organic alignment. By establishing streamlined indexing for local initiatives, we give residents in Singapore the structural framework needed to find each other, execute collaborative education, and sustain economic resiliency independent of centralized big-tech platforms.

Our Principles

  • ✨ Contribution > Attention: We reward helping, teaching, and organizing instead of popularity.
  • 👥 Communities > Audiences: The primary object is the community, not the individual.
  • 🤝 Reciprocity > Consumption: Every interaction is an opportunity to offer and receive.
  • 📍 Local First: Discover collaborators and projects in your neighborhood before the global noise.
